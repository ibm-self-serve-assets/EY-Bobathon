GOVERNMENT NOTIFICATION
Notification No. 14/2024 — Central Tax (Rate)
Subject: Amendment to GST rates and exemptions for specified goods and services

In exercise of powers conferred under the Central Goods and Services Tax Act,
the Government makes the following amendments, effective from 1st July 2024:

1. ENTRY 25 — Renewable Energy Devices
   Supply of solar water heaters and solar power generating systems shall
   attract GST at the rate of 12%, applicable across all States and Union
   Territories. This supersedes the earlier rate of 5% prescribed under
   Notification 1/2017, which stood withdrawn with effect from 1st July 2024.

2. ENTRY 26 — Educational Services
   Services provided by an educational institution to its students, faculty,
   and staff by way of education up to higher secondary school or equivalent
   shall continue to be exempt from GST. This exemption does not extend to
   services provided by such institutions by way of conducting entrance
   examinations against a separately charged fee, which shall attract GST
   at 18%.

3. ENTRY 27 — Reverse Charge on Legal Services
   Where any service is supplied by an individual advocate or a firm of
   advocates by way of legal services to a business entity located in the
   taxable territory, the recipient of such service shall be liable to pay
   GST under the reverse charge mechanism, provided the business entity has
   an aggregate turnover exceeding the threshold limit for registration
   under the Act. Where the recipient's turnover is below this threshold,
   the supplier (advocate) shall be liable to pay GST in the normal manner
   at 18%.

4. ENTRY 28 — Food and Beverage Services
   Supply of food or beverages by a restaurant, including takeaway and room
   service, shall attract GST at 5% without the benefit of input tax credit,
   except where such restaurant is located within the premises of a
   specified hotel where the value of supply of any unit of accommodation
   exceeds Rs. 7,500 per day, in which case the rate applicable shall be 18%
   with the benefit of input tax credit.

5. ENTRY 29 — Goods Transport Agency Services
   Services provided by a Goods Transport Agency (GTA) in respect of
   transportation of goods by road shall be subject to GST at 5% without
   input tax credit, payable under reverse charge by the recipient where
   the recipient is a registered person. Where the GTA opts to pay GST under
   forward charge, the rate shall be 12% with input tax credit, and such
   option once exercised shall apply for the entire financial year.

This notification shall come into force on the 1st day of July, 2024.
